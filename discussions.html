Discussions

The Border Topologies Podcast unpacks and analyses methods of governance and securitisation along border zones, investigating their impact on marginalised populations. The podcast is centered on a series of discussions which examine topics ranging from technology and infrastructure to policy and political relations as well as the lasting cultural effects of border enforcement. Over the course of the series we bring expertise from our own research and engage in conversation with other researchers and activists whose work focuses on key issues along border regions.

Episode One: Border Infrastructures

6th December 2020

In this episode of Border Topologies we discuss temporary courthouses built for asylum hearings along the US-Mexico border in Texas and architectures of surveillance and detention in Xinjiang investigating the practices and effects of border infrastructures at each of these sites.

Episode Two: The immaterial border - Surveillance as social fabric

11th December 2020

In this episode of border topologies we discuss the immaterial aspects of structural violence at border zones examining the social relations, policy and political ideology that support operations at the border.
